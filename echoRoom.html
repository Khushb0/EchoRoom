    Eating healthy food is one of the simplest ways to support mental health. Nutrient-rich foods like fruits, vegetables, whole grains, nuts, and fish provide essential vitamins and minerals that help the brain produce serotonin and dopamine — the chemicals responsible for good mood and emotional balance.

  • something you love -

    Other things may improve your mental health, but what truly makes you happy is doing what you love — moments like dancing, performing magic, running, gazing at the stars and thinking, talking to yourself, praising yourself, or anything else that brings you joy will calm your mind.

  • Gratitude journaling -

    Sometimes, in the delusion of sadness, stress, or depression, we forget that we already have so many things that others may not — like eyes to see this world, a phone in our hands, a functioning body, and a life to live. Writing a gratitude journal when you feel lost or think you have nothing can help you realize how worthy and blessed you truly are.
